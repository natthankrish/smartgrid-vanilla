Green Energy

Renewable Energy Penetration

Leveraging Performance Evaluation Through Special Maintenance of Photovoltaic Power Plant in Nusa Tenggara Region

The maintenance and performance evaluation of PV power plants in the Nusa Tenggara region is using special maintenance programs that have been implemented to enhance the reliability and efficiency of PV modules, thereby increasing the penetration of renewable energy in PLN's power network.

Cirata Green Ecosystem

This project integrates various renewable energy sources, including hydro and solar power plants, to create a comprehensive green ecosystem. The Cirata Green Ecosystem enhances renewable energy penetration by combining energy forestry and biomass machine installations, supporting PLN's goal of increasing the share of clean energy in its power generation mix. This initiative highlights PLN's commitment to sustainable energy development and environmental stewardship.

FPV Cirata 145MWac Project

This project involves the development of a floating solar power plant at Cirata Reservoir, which will be the largest in Southeast Asia. It supports PLN's goal to increase renewable energy penetration in Indonesia's energy mix.

Reverse Engineering Nule Solar Power Plant

The project involves the development of a hybrid solar power plant on Pantar Island, integrating solar energy with battery storage and diesel generators. This initiative aims to maximize local renewable energy potential, reduce fuel consumption, and lower CO2 emissions, contributing to PLN’s goal of increasing renewable energy penetration in Indonesia’s energy mix.

EV Facilitation

PLN Initiatives for EV Facilitation

PLN is actively incentivizing the adoption and support of electric vehicles (EVs) through a series of strategic initiatives. These include offering reduced connection fees and power change incentives for owners of 4-wheeled KBLBB with home charging setups, as well as for SPKLU and SPBKLU business entities, fostering the growth of charging infrastructure providers. Additionally, PLN offers a 30% discount on electricity tariffs for home chargers used between 22.00-05.00, which helps to reduce peak loads and encourage off-peak charging. The use of electric vehicles within PLN's engineering services fleet further demonstrates their commitment to green energy. Partnerships are being formed to facilitate the provision of SPKLU infrastructure, creating opportunities for non-IUPTL business entities to invest. The construction of various charging stations, from medium to ultra-fast charging, is underway at rest areas, offices, and public facilities across Indonesia. PLN also provides a comprehensive EV Digital Service platform that includes features for charging, battery exchange, an EV marketplace, travel planning, home charger settings, and fostering an EV community. Lastly, SPLU and SPBKLU are being widely deployed throughout the country to support this growing ecosystem.
